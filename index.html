What to actually do about it

For Zomato

The Budget & Lower-Sentiment segment is the largest group (42 restaurants) and the lowest-rated — the highest-ROI target for a focused quality-improvement outreach, rather than a platform-wide initiative. The Budget-Mid segment is an under-merchandised "great value" opportunity for a curated collection.

For restaurant owners

Landing in the lower-sentiment segment points to a service/consistency issue, not a pricing one — the gap is driven more by review tone than cost. Budget-Mid restaurants have room to test modest price increases without losing their value positioning.

For diners

"Top-rated / occasion dining" recommendations should draw from the Premium segment. "Best value nearby" should draw from Budget-Mid. Segments are re-computed periodically, so a restaurant's placement can shift as new reviews arrive.

The fine print

Based on ~100 Hyderabad restaurants — a small, single-city sample, so segment boundaries shouldn't be reused as-is for another city. A resampling stability check found moderate (not high) consistency (mean Adjusted Rand Index 0.664): broad patterns are trustworthy, but any single restaurant near a segment boundary should be read as provisional. Sentiment is scored with a general-purpose lexicon (VADER), not one trained specifically on restaurant reviews. Segments are driven primarily by review-text content and cuisine identity rather than price directly — the price/quality pattern shown here is a real but secondary effect.
